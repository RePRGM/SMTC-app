Why going to class
isn’t making you better

This might sound uncomfortable — but most dancers already know it’s true. You go to class. You go to socials. You go to festivals. You dance a lot. And yet… something still feels missing.

Your timing feels inconsistent. Certain accents never quite land. You know when someone else looks clean — but you can’t always explain why you don’t.

The problem isn’t effort.

The problem is how you’re practicing.

Classes are for learning choreography.
Socials are for expression and connection.
Festivals are for inspiration.

None of those are designed for deliberate practice.

Most dancers don’t know what to practice

You know you should practice — but practice what? The whole song? The whole routine? Just dancing more?

Real improvement happens in the details: the 8-count where the groove changes, the accent you keep missing, the transition that feels rushed.

This app exists to fix that gap

This isn’t a choreography app. It’s not a class replacement. It’s a training tool for dancers who want to practice with intention.

Mark the exact moment you want to work on.
Loop it without breaking flow.
Slow it down to understand it.
Speed it back up to own it.

It turns vague practice into focused reps. It turns musical awareness into muscle memory. It gives you a clear answer to “what should I practice today?”

Built from real frustration

Show Me the Counts exists because writing timestamps in Notes and scrubbing through Spotify is ridiculous — but it’s what dancers have been forced to do for years.

This is the missing layer between class and the dance floor.
