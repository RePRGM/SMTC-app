Why dancing more
isn’t making you better

You go to class. You go to socials. You go to festivals. You dance a lot. And yet… something still feels missing.

Your timing feels inconsistent. Certain accents never quite land. You know when someone else looks clean — but you can’t always explain why you don’t.

Classes are the first step — where you’re exposed to new movements, combinations, and ideas.
It’s where you ask questions, get feedback, and get reps in with other dancers.
Socials are for expression and connection.
Festivals are for inspiration.

But none of those are designed for deliberate, focused practice.

Most dancers don’t know
what to practice

Real improvement happens in the details — the 8-count where the groove changes, the accent you keep missing, the transition that feels rushed.

Mark the exact moment you want to work on.
Loop it without breaking flow.
Slow it down to understand it.
Speed it back up to own it.

Show Me the Counts turns vague practice into focused reps. It gives you a clear answer to “what should I practice today?”
